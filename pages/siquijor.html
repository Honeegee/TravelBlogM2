About Siquijor

Siquijor, often referred to as the "Island of Fire," is a captivating destination in the Philippines. Nestled in the heart of the Visayas, Siquijor is a small island known for its enchanting waterfalls, pristine beaches, and rich folklore. Surrounded by the vibrant blue waters of the Visayan Sea, this island invites travelers to explore its lush landscapes, experience its mystical traditions, and bask in its natural beauty.

The name "Island of Fire" has historical significance, originating from the sight of flickering lights seen by early Spanish explorers who thought the island was enchanted. Today, it still holds a sense of mystery and allure, drawing visitors eager to uncover its secrets. Siquijor's charm lies not only in its stunning scenery but also in its rich cultural heritage. The island is steeped in local traditions, where ancient healing practices intertwine with everyday life, creating a unique atmosphere that beckons travelers.

Top Attractions

  • Salagdoong Beach - Renowned for its powdery white sand and crystal-clear waters, Salagdoong Beach is a must-visit for beach lovers. This beach is famous for its cliff diving spots, where thrill-seekers can take exhilarating leaps into the turquoise waters below. The beach is framed by rugged cliffs and lush vegetation, creating a stunning backdrop for sunbathing and swimming. Visitors can unwind on the soft sand or explore the vibrant marine life by snorkeling in the nearby coral reefs. Salagdoong Beach also features charming beachside huts, perfect for enjoying a meal or refreshing drink while soaking in the breathtaking ocean views.
  • Enchanted Balete Tree - This centuries-old tree, located in the town of Lazi, is a fascinating site steeped in local folklore. The Balete Tree is believed to possess mystical properties, and many locals view it as a sacred site. Visitors can sit beneath its sprawling branches and enjoy the tranquility of the area. Nearby, a natural spring provides a unique opportunity for foot spa treatments, where tiny fish nibble on your feet, creating a relaxing experience. The Enchanted Balete Tree serves as a reminder of Siquijor's rich cultural heritage and the deep-rooted beliefs that continue to thrive on the island.

Practical Travel Information

Getting There: Traveling to Siquijor is relatively easy, with various transportation options available. Ferries operate regularly from major cities such as Cebu and Dumaguete, making the journey enjoyable with scenic views of the surrounding sea. The ferry ride typically takes about an hour, providing a pleasant start to your Siquijor adventure. For those who prefer flying, Siquijor has a small airport with domestic flights from Cebu and Manila. Upon arrival, visitors can easily rent scooters or take local transportation to explore the island's attractions.

Best Time to Visit: The best time to visit Siquijor is during the dry season, which spans from December to May. During these months, the weather is characterized by sunny days and minimal rainfall, making it ideal for outdoor activities such as beach hopping, hiking, and exploring the waterfalls. The dry season also coincides with various local festivals, allowing visitors to immerse themselves in the island's vibrant culture. If you prefer a quieter experience, consider visiting during the shoulder months of November or early June when the crowds are fewer, and the weather remains pleasant.

Local Cuisine: Siquijorâ€™s culinary offerings are a delightful reflection of its cultural heritage. To truly experience the island's flavors, be sure to try traditional dishes such as "adobong puti", a unique variation of the classic Filipino adobo made with pork and vinegar, resulting in a savory yet slightly tangy flavor. Another local favorite is "torta," a delicious cake made from eggs, sugar, and flour, often enjoyed as a snack or dessert. Visitors can find these dishes in local eateries and markets, where they can also sample fresh seafood and tropical fruits. Exploring Siquijor's cuisine is an essential part of experiencing the island's rich culture.
