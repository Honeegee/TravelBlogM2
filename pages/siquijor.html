Siquijor is famous for:

  • Mystical Heritage: Known as the "Island of Fire," Siquijor is renowned for its traditional healers, mystical practices, and ancient healing arts that continue to intrigue visitors from around the world.
  • Natural Wonders: From the multi-tiered Cambugahay Falls to the pristine white sand beaches of Paliton and Salagdoong, the island is a haven for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

About Siquijor

Siquijor, a small island province in the Central Visayas region of the Philippines, is often referred to as the 'Mystical Island'. The island’s name has long been associated with legends and supernatural stories, believed by locals and visitors alike. Historically known as 'Isla del Fuego' or Island of Fire, this was due to the magical glow from the countless fireflies that illuminated the trees at night, casting an otherworldly light over the island. However, the real magic of Siquijor lies in its natural beauty and its unique culture.

Exploring the island reveals stunning inland natural wonders as well. At Cambugahay Falls, a multi-tiered waterfall cascades into clear pools where visitors can swim and enjoy the cool, fresh water. There’s also the mysterious Balete Tree, a centuries-old tree with an eerie reputation, where a natural spring flows at its base, providing an unusual fish spa experience.

For those interested in culture, Siquijor has a rich tradition of healing arts. Known for its folk healing practices, the island draws curious tourists during the annual 'Healing Festival', where traditional healers, called 'mananambal', gather to prepare herbal medicines and offer rituals. This aspect of the island's culture has made it a hub for those interested in alternative medicine, spiritual healing, and local folklore.

Despite its mystic reputation, Siquijor is a safe, welcoming, and peaceful island. Visitors will find its local population warm and friendly, always ready to share stories about their island's unique heritage. The island’s compact size and well-maintained coastal road make it ideal for exploration. Rent a scooter or bicycle, and within a day, you can circle the entire island, stopping at various attractions like the centuries-old St. Francis of Assisi Church, the Lazi Convent, or enjoying the scenic coastal views at every turn.

Whether you're in search of adventure, cultural immersion, or simple relaxation, Siquijor offers an unforgettable experience. It is a destination that seamlessly blends history, legend, and natural beauty, providing visitors with the perfect island escape. Siquijor is not just a place to visit; it’s a journey into the Philippines’ rich cultural past and a step into the serene and enchanting beauty of island life.

Top Attractions

  • Cambugahay Falls - A three-tiered waterfall system featuring crystal-clear turquoise waters. Visitors can enjoy swimming in the natural pools and swinging from the famous rope swings. The falls are surrounded by lush vegetation and offer a refreshing escape from the tropical heat.
  • Paliton Beach - Often called the "Little Boracay" of Siquijor, this pristine white sand beach offers spectacular sunset views and excellent swimming conditions. The beach remains relatively undeveloped, preserving its natural beauty.
  • San Isidro Labrador Church and Convent - A historic church built in 1884 featuring coral stone walls and unique architectural elements. The adjacent convent is one of the largest and oldest in the Philippines.
  • Salagdoong Beach - Famous for its cliff diving platforms and crystal-clear waters. The beach resort offers various activities including diving boards at different heights, and a man-made forest surrounds the area.
  • Century-Old Balete Tree - A mystical 400-year-old balete tree believed to be enchanted. A natural spring flows from its roots, creating a fish spa where visitors can have their feet cleaned by small fish.
  • Mt. Bandilaan Nature Park - The highest point on the island, offering hiking trails and a butterfly sanctuary. The park is also known for its healing herbs and traditional medicine.
  • Tulapos Marine Sanctuary - A protected marine area perfect for snorkeling and diving, featuring diverse coral formations and marine life.
  • Lazi Church and Convent - A National Cultural Treasure featuring impressive architecture and historical artifacts. The convent is one of Asia's largest dating back to the Spanish colonial period.
  • Hapitanan Mountain View - A unique attraction featuring various wooden swings and photo spots with panoramic views of the island.
  • Lugnason Falls - A secluded waterfall offering a peaceful swimming experience away from the crowds, surrounded by tropical forest.

Practical Travel Information

Getting There: Access to Siquijor is primarily via ferry from Dumaguete, Cebu, or Bohol. The nearest airports are Dumaguete Airport and Tagbilaran Airport. Regular ferry services operate throughout the day from these major ports.

Best Time to Visit: The best time to visit is during the dry season from December to May. March to May are the hottest months, while June to November may experience occasional rainfall. The Holy Week period is particularly interesting for witnessing traditional healing practices.

Local Cuisine: Siquijor offers fresh seafood, traditional Filipino dishes, and local specialties. Must-try dishes include grilled seafood, kinilaw (Filipino ceviche), and local delicacies like torta (native cake). Many beachfront restaurants serve catch-of-the-day specials.
