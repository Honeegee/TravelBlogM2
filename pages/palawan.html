Palawan is famous for:

  • Pristine Beaches: Palawan boasts some of the world's most beautiful beaches, with powdery white sand and crystal-clear waters. El Nido's hidden beaches and Coron's Twin Lagoon are just a few examples of the stunning coastal landscapes.
  • Underground River: The Puerto Princesa Subterranean River National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, features a spectacular limestone karst landscape with an underground river that emerges directly into the sea.
  • Rich Marine Life: Palawan's waters are teeming with diverse marine life, making it a paradise for snorkeling and diving enthusiasts. The Tubbataha Reefs Natural Park, another UNESCO site, is home to a vast array of marine species.

About Palawan

Palawan, often referred to as the "Last Frontier of the Philippines," is a breathtaking archipelagic province located in the western part of the country. Stretching from Mindoro to Borneo, this long, narrow province is home to some of the most stunning natural wonders in the world. Palawan is renowned for its pristine beaches, limestone cliffs, and crystal-clear waters that harbor an abundance of marine life.

The province is not just a haven for beach lovers and diving enthusiasts; it's also rich in biodiversity, hosting two UNESCO World Heritage Sites: the Tubbataha Reefs Natural Park and the Puerto Princesa Subterranean River National Park. Palawan's commitment to environmental conservation has earned it recognition as one of the best island destinations globally, repeatedly topping travel magazine lists and capturing the hearts of travelers worldwide.

Top Attractions

  • El Nido - Known for its stunning limestone cliffs, hidden lagoons, and secluded beaches, El Nido is a paradise for island-hopping enthusiasts. The Big and Small Lagoons offer surreal landscapes where emerald waters are enclosed by towering limestone formations. Visitors can explore these natural wonders by kayak or small boat, immersing themselves in the breathtaking scenery. The nearby islands of Miniloc, Shimizu, and Matinloc offer excellent snorkeling and diving opportunities, with vibrant coral reefs and diverse marine life.
  • Puerto Princesa Underground River - This UNESCO World Heritage Site is one of the world's longest navigable underground rivers. Visitors can take a boat tour through the cave system, marveling at the unique rock formations, expansive caverns, and diverse ecosystem within. The surrounding national park also offers opportunities for hiking, birdwatching, and spotting unique wildlife like monitor lizards and long-tailed macaques.
  • Coron Island - Famous for its world-class diving sites, Coron is a must-visit for underwater enthusiasts. The island is home to several Japanese shipwrecks from World War II, now serving as artificial reefs teeming with marine life. Kayangan Lake, often called the cleanest lake in Asia, offers crystal-clear waters perfect for swimming and snorkeling. The Twin Lagoons, separated by a small crevice in the limestone cliffs, provide a unique swimming experience where cool freshwater meets warm saltwater.
  • Honda Bay - Located near Puerto Princesa City, Honda Bay is perfect for a day of island-hopping. Visitors can explore several small islands, each with its own charm. Starfish Island is known for its abundance of starfish in the shallow waters, while Luli Island features a sandbar that appears and disappears with the tides. Cowrie Island offers various water activities and is an ideal spot for relaxation and sunbathing.

Practical Travel Information

Getting There: Palawan is accessible via air travel, with three main airports serving the province: Puerto Princesa International Airport, El Nido Airport, and Francisco B. Reyes Airport in Busuanga (for Coron). Regular flights operate from major cities in the Philippines, including Manila and Cebu. Once on the island, visitors can use local transportation such as tricycles, vans, or boats to reach their destinations. For inter-island travel, ferry services are available between major towns.

Best Time to Visit: The best time to visit Palawan is during the dry season, which runs from November to May. This period offers the most favorable weather conditions for outdoor activities and island-hopping tours. The peak tourist season is from December to April, coinciding with the coolest and driest months. If you prefer fewer crowds and don't mind occasional rain showers, the shoulder months of June and October can also be good times to visit, often offering lower prices on accommodations and activities.

Local Cuisine: Palawan's cuisine is a delightful mix of Filipino flavors with influences from its proximity to Borneo. Seafood is a staple in many local dishes, given the province's abundant marine resources. Must-try dishes include "Tamilok" (woodworm), a local delicacy for the adventurous eater, and "Chao Long," a Vietnamese-inspired noodle soup popular in Puerto Princesa. Fresh coconut water and tropical fruits are readily available and perfect for refreshment in the tropical climate. For a unique dining experience, try the "Boodle Fight," a traditional Filipino feast where various dishes are laid out on banana leaves and eaten with hands, often enjoyed on island-hopping tours.
